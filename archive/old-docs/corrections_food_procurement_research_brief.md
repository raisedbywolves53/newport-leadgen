## 1. FEDERAL BUREAU OF PRISONS (BOP)

### Overview
The Federal Bureau of Prisons operates 122 federal institutions housing ~155,000 inmates. BOP **self-operates** food service at its facilities using inmate labor under staff supervision. Food procurement is handled through centralized and regional acquisition offices.

### How BOP Procures Food
- **Centralized procurement** through the Central Office Acquisition Branch in Washington, DC, which solicits, awards, and administers national contracts for all Bureau facilities.
- **Regional procurement** through six regional offices that handle local purchases.
- **Individual facility procurement** for smaller orders (e.g., fresh produce, perishables).
- All open market items exceeding **$25,000** must be posted to **SAM.gov** (System for Award Management).
- BOP uses a **national standardized menu** (published annually as "National Menu").
- The BOP solicits **subsistence contracts** for categories like meat/protein, produce, dairy, dry goods, and frozen foods, often on a quarterly or annual basis.
- BOP is required to purchase from **UNICOR/Federal Prison Industries** when available, and to use mandatory government sources (e.g., AbilityOne, GSA schedules) before open market purchasing.

### Key BOP Food Service Organizational Structure
| Level | Title | Role |
|-------|-------|------|
| Central Office (Washington, DC) | **National Food and Farm Services Administrator (NFSA)** | Oversees entire BOP food program, sets national policy, menu standards |
| Regional Office (6 regions) | **Regional Food Service Administrator (RFSA)** | Oversees food service for all institutions in the region |
| Individual Institution | **Food Service Administrator (FSA)** | Manages day-to-day food operations at each facility |
| Central Office | **Chief, Acquisitions Branch** | Oversees all national procurement contracts |
| Regional Office | **Supervisory Contract Specialist** | Manages regional procurement actions |

### BOP Procurement Contact Points
- **BOP Central Office:** 320 First Street NW, Washington, DC 20534 | (202) 307-3082
- **BOP Business Website:** https://www.bop.gov/business/
- **Solicitations & Awards:** https://www.bop.gov/business/solicitations.jsp
- **BOP Contact Directory:** https://www.bop.gov/business/bop_directory.jsp
- **SAM.gov** - All federal solicitations exceeding $25,000

### BOP Regional Offices (Each Has Procurement Staff)
1. **Northeast Region** - Philadelphia, PA
2. **Mid-Atlantic Region** - Annapolis Junction, MD
3. **Southeast Region** - Atlanta, GA
4. **North Central Region** - Kansas City, KS
5. **South Central Region** - Dallas, TX
6. **Western Region** - Stockton, CA

### UNICOR / Federal Prison Industries
- UNICOR is a self-sustaining government corporation within BOP.
- UNICOR does **not** directly handle food procurement; that is a separate BOP function.
- UNICOR operates manufacturing programs and pays inmate wages ($0.23-$1.15/hour).
- Inmates use wages to purchase food and personal items from the **commissary**.
- BOP commissaries are operated internally by BOP staff.

### Newport Opportunity
- Newport can bid on BOP subsistence contracts via **SAM.gov**.
- Focus on individual institution-level purchases for perishable/fresh products.
- Regional offices are key targets for regional supply contracts.
- Must register as a government vendor on SAM.gov and obtain a CAGE code.
- HUBZone, small business, and veteran-owned certifications provide competitive advantage.

## 2. STATE DEPARTMENTS OF CORRECTIONS

### Overview: Self-Operated vs. Contracted Out
The 50 state DOCs represent the largest segment of corrections food procurement. States fall into two categories:

**Model A: Self-Operated Food Service** - The state DOC runs its own kitchens with state employees and/or inmate labor, and procures food directly through state purchasing offices.

**Model B: Contracted Food Service** - The state DOC outsources food service management to a private company (Aramark, Trinity, Summit, etc.), which then handles food procurement.

### How Self-Operated States Procure Food
1. **Centralized state purchasing** through the state's General Services / Procurement office (e.g., California uses DGS)
2. **Statewide contracts** awarded through competitive bidding (RFPs/ITBs)
3. **Delegated purchasing authority** for individual facilities (typically for fresh/perishable items)
4. **State commodity programs** (e.g., California uses CALPIA for certain products)
5. **USDA commodity programs** - States receive USDA commodities through school/institutional food programs

1. **Centralized corporate procurement** - Corporate office negotiates national/regional supplier contracts.
