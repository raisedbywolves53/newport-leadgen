- **Bids:** 20-25 micro-purchases + 3-5 simplified

## 5. Product Categories — Newport's Competitive Advantage

### Tier 1: Highest Priority (Play to Newport's Strengths)

**Confectionery & Nuts (PSC 8925)** — $55M federal, $412K FL
- Directly aligned with Newport's candy wholesale expertise
- Only 45 NAICS 424450 awards nationally, 1.6 avg offers, 58% sole-source
- Massively underserved in FL — almost no competition
- **Action: This is Newport's beachhead**

**Fresh Produce (PSC 8915)** — $2.34B federal, $35.1M FL
- Lowest vendor concentration federally
- USDA actively diversifying vendor base
- FL location ideal for sourcing
- Oakes Farms doing $26M — proves the model works

### Tier 2: Growth Targets

**Dairy & Eggs (PSC 8910)** — $693M federal, $5.5M FL
- Regional distribution feasible
- Fluid milk and eggs accessible for wholesale distributors

**Bakery & Cereal (PSC 8920)** — $541M federal, $2.5M FL
- Covered by NAICS 424410, regional vendors compete well

**General Grocery (PSC 8945 + 8940)** — $1B federal, $13.5M FL
- Broader category including oils, fats, specialty foods
- Good fit for general line wholesale

### Tier 3: Avoid / Long-Term Only

**Meat, Poultry, Fish (PSC 8905)** — Dominated by Tyson/JBS/Cargill
**MREs/Composite (PSC 8970)** — Specialized military manufacturing
**Beverages (PSC 8960)** — Low margin, high logistics cost

### Pricing Advantage Analysis

| Product Category | Typical Gov Vendor Markup | Newport Wholesale Advantage |
|-----------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Confectionery & Snacks | 20-30% over wholesale | **Existing Segment E supplier pricing** |
| Fresh Produce | 15-25% over wholesale | Direct sourcing at wholesale cost |
| Dairy & Eggs | 12-20% markup | Regional distribution, lower delivery cost |
| General Grocery | 15-25% markup | Volume purchasing, existing logistics |

Most food contracts use LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable) — **this directly favors wholesale distributors with existing volume purchasing.**

## 6. Consideration Questions for Newport Ownership

### A. Distribution & Logistics (CRITICAL — Determines Geographic Scope)

1. **What states do your trucks currently deliver to on a regular basis?**
   - FL only? FL + neighboring states? How far north/west?
2. **What are your current delivery routes?**
   - Which cities/regions are on your regular schedule?
   - What is your delivery radius from the South FL warehouse?
3. **Do you have refrigerated/cold chain trucks?**
   - Temperature-controlled vehicles are required for most food contracts
   - What temperature ranges can you maintain?
4. **What is your minimum order size for delivery?**
   - Government micro-purchases can be small ($3K-$15K) — is this worth a truck run?
   - Can you consolidate government deliveries with commercial routes?
5. **Do you have relationships with distribution partners in other states?**
   - Could you subcontract delivery outside your service area?
6. **What is your warehouse capacity?**
   - Could you take on 20-30 additional small government orders per month?
   - Any seasonal capacity constraints?

### B. Product & Supply Chain

7. **What product categories do you currently wholesale?**
   - Candy/confectionery, produce, dairy, general grocery, snacks?
   - Which are your highest-volume and best-margin categories?
8. **Which products can you source at below-market pricing?**
   - Where does Newport have the strongest supplier relationships?
   - Any exclusive or preferred supplier agreements?
9. **Can you handle USDA commodity distribution?**
   - This requires HACCP/food safety compliance
   - Do you currently have any food safety certifications?
10. **What is your product liability insurance coverage?**
    - Government contracts may require higher limits
    - Current policy limits and carriers?

### C. Financial & Operational

11. **What is your current gross margin on wholesale distribution?**
    - We're assuming 11% — is this accurate for government-type deliveries?
12. **Can you handle NET 30 or NET 60 payment terms?**
    - Federal payments average NET 30; state/local can be NET 60-90
    - What is your cash flow position for carrying receivables?
13. **Do you have bonding capacity?**
    - Most small food contracts do NOT require surety bonds
    - But some larger contracts ($100K+) may require performance bonds
14. **Who would manage government contract administration?**
    - Someone needs to track deadlines, compliance, reporting
    - Is this an existing employee or does it need to be hired?

### D. Certifications & Registrations

15. **Is Newport currently registered in SAM.gov?**
    - If not, this is step 1 — free, takes 2-4 weeks
16. **Does Newport qualify for any SBA set-aside programs?**
    - Small business? (likely yes, by revenue)
    - Minority-owned? Veteran-owned? HUBZone? Woman-owned?
    - These dramatically increase win probability on set-aside contracts
17. **Does Newport have any existing government contracts or past performance?**
    - State/local contracts count as past performance
    - Any relationship with military bases, schools, or government facilities?
18. **Does Newport have HACCP or other food safety certifications?**
    - Required for certain USDA programs
    - FDA facility registration?

### E. Strategic Intent

19. **How much time/resources is ownership willing to commit in Year 1?**
    - Part-time BD effort (10 hrs/week) vs. dedicated person
    - This directly determines bid volume and scenario outcome
20. **What is the target revenue from government contracts in Year 1?**
    - Setting realistic expectations: $50K-$350K depending on effort
21. **Is Newport open to SLED (state/local) contracts as well as federal?**
    - School districts and corrections are higher volume but different procurement process
22. **Would Newport consider partnering or subcontracting to build past performance?**
    - E.g., supply to a prime contractor who has the government relationship
23. **What is the 3-5 year vision?**
    - Supplemental revenue channel? Or meaningful revenue line?
    - This determines investment level
