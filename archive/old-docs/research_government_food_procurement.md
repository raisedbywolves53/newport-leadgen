# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. government is one of the largest food purchasers in the world. Across federal, state, and local levels, government entities spend **tens of billions of dollars annually** on food procurement for military installations, correctional facilities, schools, hospitals, veterans' facilities, tribal programs, and more.

**Key facts for Newport:**
- USDA's Agricultural Marketing Service (AMS) and the Department of Defense's Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) account for **more than 90% of all federal purchases of domestic food** (GAO-24-106602).
- The Federal Bureau of Prisons alone spends approximately **$401 million annually** on food, serving ~479,000 meals per day.
- The VA Subsistence Prime Vendor contract covers 171 VA Medical Centers and ~135 other government facilities, with annual food sales of approximately **$263 million**.
- The Defense Commissary Agency (DeCA) operates **236 commissaries worldwide**, functioning as a full grocery store system for military families.
- The Urban School Food Alliance's founding members alone procure **$530+ million** in food annually, serving 3 million students daily.
- State Departments of Corrections are among the largest single-entity food purchasers at the state level (e.g., California CDCR is the largest state food buyer in CA).

**The opportunity:** Newport can sell bulk/wholesale food products at multiple levels -- directly to government agencies, through prime vendor programs, through GPOs, and through the food service management companies (Aramark, Sodexo, Compass Group) that manage food operations for many of these institutions.

# FEDERAL LEVEL

## 1. USDA -- Agricultural Marketing Service (AMS), Commodity Procurement

**What they buy:** Domestically produced and processed commodity food products including dairy, meat, poultry, fish, fruits, vegetables, grains, cereals, pasta, bakery, peanut products, and vegetable oil products.

**Scale:** AMS is one of the two largest federal food purchasers. Their purchases supply four major FNS distribution programs:
- **TEFAP** (The Emergency Food Assistance Program) -- food banks and food pantries
- **CSFP** (Commodity Supplemental Food Program) -- low-income elderly
- **FDPIR** (Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations) -- 110 tribal agencies
- **USDA Foods in Schools** -- National School Lunch Program

**How procurement works:**
- AMS publishes a **Master Solicitation for Commodity Procurements** (current version effective November 19, 2025)
- Vendors must become **approved/qualified** through an application process
- Competitive bidding among approved vendors
- Contracts awarded through solicitations posted on AMS website and SAM.gov

**How to become a vendor:**
1. Subscribe to AMS CP News for solicitation notifications
2. Review the Master Solicitation for Commodity Procurement
3. Review Current Purchase Schedules (separate schedules for produce/protein vs. dairy/bakery/grains)
4. Review commodity specifications and technical approval requirements
5. Complete administrative application: Active SAM registration, WBSCM Vendor Registration, company letter, three customer references
6. Non-manufacturers must submit supplier letters

**Key contacts:**
- New Vendor/Small Business Coordinators: Diana Dau David and Chelsie Prange
- Email: NewVendor@usda.gov
- Office: USDA AMS Commodity Procurement Staff

**Website:** https://www.ams.usda.gov/selling-food/becoming-approved

## 2. Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) -- Troop Support, Subsistence Directorate

**What they buy:** All food for U.S. military dining facilities worldwide -- from individually packaged meals (MREs) to full-service dining facility supplies, fresh produce, frozen foods, and dry goods.

**Scale:** DLA Troop Support Subsistence is one of the two largest federal food purchasers (alongside AMS), managing the entire military food supply chain globally.

**Key programs:**
- **Subsistence Prime Vendor (SPV) Program** -- The primary mechanism. Prime vendors deliver food within 48 hours of order placement. Individual installations order directly from distributors electronically.
- **Direct Vendor Delivery** -- For items not covered by prime vendors
- **USDA DoD Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program** -- Partnership between USDA and DLA providing fresh produce to schools in 48 states, DC, Guam, Puerto Rico, and USVI. Schools use USDA Foods entitlement dollars to buy fresh produce through DLA's supply chain.

**How procurement works:**
- Contracts posted on SAM.gov
- Prime vendors (large distributors like Sysco, US Foods) carry the products and deliver to bases
- Individual food manufacturers/wholesalers can become approved suppliers to the prime vendors
- DLA Troop Support website: https://www.dla.mil/Troop-Support/Subsistence/Working-with-Subsistence/

**Key decision-maker titles at DLA:**
- Contracting Officer
- Contracting Specialist
- Subsistence Supply Chain Director
- Program Manager, Subsistence

## 3. Defense Commissary Agency (DeCA)

**What they buy:** Full grocery store inventory -- any product sold commercially in grocery stores is viable for commissary sales. DeCA operates **236 commissaries worldwide** serving military families.

**Scale:** This is essentially a parallel grocery retail chain for the military community, representing a massive wholesale opportunity.

**How procurement works:**
- Vendors submit **DeCA Form 40-15** (Display Presentation & New Item Form)
- National distribution presentations made at DeCA HQ, Fort Gregg-Adams, Virginia
- Regional/local distribution presentations at regional offices
- **DeCA does NOT have its own distribution system** -- vendors must provide delivery consistent with commercial grocery standards
- Individual commissaries place orders directly with distributors, usually electronically
- Military brokers are commonly used as intermediaries

**Key intermediary: American Logistics Association (ALA)**
- Trade association for the military resale marketplace (~$19 billion market)
- Members include manufacturers, brokers, distributors, and service providers
- Hosts events where club managers and buyers meet 250+ manufacturers
- Essential networking organization for selling into the military channel
- Website: https://alanational.org

**DeCA vendor portal:** https://corp.commissaries.com/our-agency/business-with-deca

## 4. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) -- Nutrition and Food Services

**What they buy:** All food, beverages, and food service supplies for VA medical centers, Veterans Canteen Service operations, and other eligible government facilities.

**Scale:**
- 171 VA Medical Centers
- 160 Veterans Canteen Service operations
- 135+ other government agency facilities
- Annual food sales under the SPV contract: approximately **$263 million**

**How procurement works:**
- **Subsistence Prime Vendor (SPV) contract** -- Currently held by **US Foods, Inc.** (contract period: November 1, 2023 through October 31, 2028). This is an Indefinite-Delivery/Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ) contract.
- SPV covers all food and food service supplies EXCEPT fresh bread, fresh milk, and fresh produce
- **Local sourcing for dairy, bread, and produce** -- Purchase agreements negotiated by local or VISN (Veterans Integrated Service Network) contracting offices
- Veterans Integrated Service Networks (VISNs) serve as regional management structures

**Key decision-maker titles at VA:**
- Chief, Nutrition and Food Services
- Food Service Systems Specialist
- Contracting Officer (within OPAL -- Office of Procurement, Acquisition and Logistics)
- VISN Contracting Officer
- Dietitian/Clinical Nutrition Manager (influence purchasing specs)

**Opportunity for Newport:** While US Foods holds the national SPV, there are local/regional opportunities for fresh bread, milk, produce, and specialty items. Additionally, Newport could become a supplier TO US Foods for VA-bound products.

## 5. Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP)

**What they buy:** All food products and food services for federal correctional institutions.

**Scale:**
- 122 federal correctional institutions
- ~150,000+ inmates
- ~479,000 meals per day (~175 million meals/year)
- Annual food budget: approximately **$401 million**

**How procurement works:**
- Competitive bidding process posted on SAM.gov
- Open market items exceeding $25,000 must be posted publicly
- BOP contracts with food service management companies AND direct food suppliers
- Major FSMCs in the prison market: **Aramark Correctional Services** and **Trinity Services Group**

**Key decision-maker titles:**
- Food Service Administrator
- Contracting Officer
- Procurement Analyst
- Warden (approves major contracts)

**BOP solicitations page:** https://www.bop.gov/business/solicitations.jsp

## 6. Other Federal Food Purchasing Entities

### GSA (General Services Administration)
- Manages the **Federal Supply Schedule / Multiple Award Schedule (MAS)** program
- Food service equipment and supplies available through GSA Facilities category
- **GSA Advantage** -- Online ordering system used by many federal agencies
- **FedMall** -- Specialized marketplace where food vendors find traction

### Indian Health Service (IHS)
- Federal agency within HHS
- Operates hospitals and health facilities for American Indian/Alaska Native populations
- IHS Produce Prescription Pilot Program (P4)
- Food procurement for IHS hospitals and clinics

### FEMA / Emergency Food Procurement
- Large-scale emergency food purchases during disasters
- Contracts often let through DLA or AMS

### U.S. Coast Guard, Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines
- Individual service branches may have supplemental food procurement beyond DLA
- Base-level food service operations may purchase locally
- Army Joint Center of Excellence for Subsistence (Fort Gregg-Adams, VA) coordinates food policy

# FOOD SERVICE MANAGEMENT COMPANIES (FSMCs)

These companies manage food service operations for government institutions and are MAJOR bulk food purchasers. Selling to FSMCs is an efficient way to reach hundreds of government facilities through a single relationship.

| Company | Revenue | Government Segments | Purchasing Arm |
|---------|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Compass Group** (Chartwells, Morrison, Eurest, Unidine) | $42.2B (FY24) | Schools (Chartwells), hospitals (Morrison), government facilities (Unidine), corrections | **Foodbuy** |
| **Aramark** | $17.4B (FY24) | Corrections (largest prison food contractor), schools, hospitals, military | Internal procurement |
| **Sodexo** | $24.9B (FY24) | Schools, hospitals, government facilities, military | Internal procurement |
| **Trinity Services Group** | Private | Correctional facilities (2nd largest) | Internal procurement |
| **Elior Group / ABL Management** | Major | Schools, government | Internal procurement |
| **Delaware North** | Major | Government parks and recreation, military | Internal procurement |

**Together, Compass, Aramark, and Sodexo prepare and serve over 6 billion meals per year** and represent 77.5% of the food service management industry.

**Key insight for Newport:** Selling to Aramark, Compass/Foodbuy, Sodexo, or Trinity gives indirect access to thousands of government accounts without needing individual government contracts. These FSMCs are essentially wholesale buyers themselves.

# HOW TO GET STARTED (VENDOR REGISTRATION ROADMAP)

## Step 1: Register on SAM.gov
- Free registration, required for all federal contracts
- Get a Unique Entity ID (UEI)
- Select appropriate NAICS codes (424410, 424490, etc.)
- Select appropriate PSC codes (89xx series)
- Registration takes up to 10 business days
- Must renew every 365 days

## Step 2: Become a USDA AMS Approved Vendor
- Subscribe to AMS CP News
- Review Master Solicitation
- Complete administrative application
- Contact NewVendor@usda.gov

## Step 3: Register on Key State Procurement Portals
- Prioritize the top 10 states by food spend (TX, CA, FL, NY, PA, OH, GA, MI, IL, NC)
- Register as a vendor on each state's procurement portal
- Set up bid notification alerts for food/grocery categories

## Step 4: Connect with Intermediaries
- Join the American Logistics Association (military channel)
- Approach Foodbuy, Vizient, Premier about becoming an approved vendor
- Contact Aramark, Sodexo, Compass Group vendor applications
- Apply to become a supplier for Sysco and US Foods (they are prime vendors for many government contracts)

## Step 5: Monitor Opportunities
- Set up SAM.gov saved searches for food procurement solicitations
- Monitor USDA AMS purchase announcements
- Monitor state procurement portals
- Attend industry events (ALA conferences, SNA conferences, ACFSA conferences)
