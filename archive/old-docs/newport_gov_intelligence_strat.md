## PART 4: THE BUILD LIST (Claude CLI Projects)

### 1. SAM.gov Federal Opportunity Monitor
- Daily automated query: NAICS 424410 + 424450 + 424490
- Filter: $10K–$350K, Southeast states, active solicitations
- Output: Google Sheet with title, agency, dollar range, deadline, contracting officer contact, link
- Bonus: Also track Award Notices to see who's winning contracts in your space

### 2. USASpending TAM Analysis
- Pull all historical awards for Newport's NAICS codes
- Segment by: agency, state, contract size, fiscal year
- Answer: "What is the total addressable market for grocery wholesaling to government in the Southeast?"
- Output: Dashboard showing spend by agency, by state, by year, trends

### 3. Contract Expiration Tracker
- Pull from USASpending: all current contracts in Newport's NAICS codes with end dates
- Flag contracts expiring in 6–18 months (these will be re-competed)
- Output: Pipeline of upcoming opportunities before they hit SAM.gov

### 4. Competitor Intelligence Report
- From USASpending + GovWin: identify every company winning contracts in Newport's NAICS codes in the Southeast
- Map: who they are, what they won, from which agencies, contract size, geographic coverage
- Identify gaps: which agencies/regions are underserved?

### 5. Decision-Maker Contact Database
- Combine GovWin contacts + Apollo.io enrichment
- Target titles: Food Service Director, Dietary Manager, Procurement Specialist, Contracting Officer, Emergency Management Coordinator
- Target organizations: Every school district, DOC, VA hospital, county govt in delivery radius
- Output: Enriched CRM-ready contact list for outreach

### 6. SAM.gov Entity Management — Competitor Intelligence Registry (FREE API)
- **API**: `GET/POST https://api.sam.gov/entity-information/v3/entities`
- **Auth**: Same SAM_API_KEY we already have
- Query by `primaryNaics=424410` + `physicalAddressProvinceOrStateCode=FL` + `registrationStatus=A`
- Returns: Every registered food wholesaler in FL — UEI, CAGE code, size certifications (WOSB, HUBZone, 8(a), SDVOSB), contact info, all NAICS codes they registered under
- **This is the competitor intelligence database for free.** No paid platform needed for this.
- Expand to all 10 target SE states: FL, GA, AL, SC, NC, TN, MS, LA, VA, TX
- Rate limits: 1,000 req/day (same key as Opportunities API)
- Output: CSV of all registered food wholesalers by state with certifications, size, contacts

### 7. FPDS — Raw Federal Contract Transactions (FREE, NO KEY)
- **Library**: `pip install fpds` — Python library that parses FPDS ATOM feed with auto-pagination and XML-to-JSON conversion
- **Endpoint**: `https://www.fpds.gov/ezsearch/FEEDS/ATOM?FEEDNAME=PUBLIC&q=`
- Query by `PRINCIPAL_NAICS_CODE`, `VENDOR_ADDRESS_STATE_CODE`, `OBLIGATED_AMOUNT` range, `SIGNED_DATE` range
- **Fields USASpending doesn't surface cleanly**: vendor CAGE codes, detailed small business certifications, contract modification history, **number of offers received per award** (competition metric)
- **Key computed metric**: Average offers per award = direct measure of competition density per NAICS/agency
- Output: Competition density report — which NAICS/agency combos have low bidder counts (easy entry) vs. crowded

### 8. Grants.gov — USDA Food Grant Monitoring (FREE, NO KEY)
- **API**: `POST https://api.grants.gov/v1/api/search2`
- Search with `fundingCategories="AR"` (Agriculture) + keywords: "food", "nutrition", "commodity", "TEFAP", "LFPA", "CSFP", "FDPIR"
- Surfaces grant opportunities for: USDA TEFAP, LFPA ($1.13B), CSFP, FDPIR, Farm to School, DoD Fresh
- These are the programs listed in Part 6 — this API automates monitoring them
- Also: `GET https://api.grants.gov/v1/api/fetchOpportunity` for full grant details
- Output: Active USDA food grant opportunities with deadlines, amounts, eligibility

### 9. USASpending Enhanced — County-Level + Recipient + Subcontract Intelligence (FREE)
Additional endpoints to add to the existing USASpending client:

| Endpoint | What It Answers |
|----------|----------------|
| `POST /search/spending_by_category/recipient/` | "Who are the top vendors winning food contracts?" — ranked by dollars, direct incumbent identification |
| `POST /search/spending_by_category/county/` | "How much does Broward County spend on federal food contracts?" — also Miami-Dade, Palm Beach, every SE FL county |
| `POST /search/spending_by_geography/` | Heat map of federal food spending by state/county — where does the money concentrate? |
| `GET /recipient/<HASH>/` | Deep profile on any specific competitor — every contract they've won, agencies served, dollar history |
| `GET /recipient/children/<UEI>/` | Competitor's subsidiaries and child entities |

| `POST /search/new_awards_over_time/` | Monthly trend — is food procurement growing or declining per NAICS? |
| `POST /subawards/` | **Subcontract data** — which primes are subcontracting food supply? Critical for the DLA Troop Support path |

### 10. Computed Market Analytics Dashboard
Metrics calculated from free API data that no paid platform provides:

| Metric | Formula | Source | What It Tells Newport |
|--------|---------|--------|----------------------|
| **Market Saturation Index** | `unique vendors with awards / total awards` per NAICS per geography | USASpending recipient data | Which food procurement niches are underserved — fewer vendors competing = easier entry |
| **Competition Density** | `avg number of offers received per award` per NAICS/agency | FPDS data (offers field) | Direct measure of bidder count. NAICS/agency with 2 avg offers = easy. 8+ = crowded. |
| **12-Month Award Trend** | Monthly award count + value from `spending_over_time` | USASpending | Is this category growing, stable, or declining? Directional signal for where to focus. |
| **Incumbent Concentration** | `top 3 vendors % of total spend` per NAICS/state | USASpending recipient data | How concentrated is the market? High concentration = harder to break in. Low = fragmented = opportunity. |
| **Contract Renewal Pipeline** | Awards with POP end dates in 6-18 months | USASpending award detail | Which specific contracts will be re-competed soon? Early positioning window. |

### 9. Government Event Catering
Every government agency holds events, conferences, training sessions that need food. These are often micro-purchases under $10K — captured by GovSpend but invisible on SAM.gov.

Defense: DLA Troop Support Subsistence Prime Vendor Program ($3.79B across 55 contracts), DeCA commissaries ($4B+ in sales), Coast Guard (SE district HQ in Miami), National Guard training exercises, and even Foreign Military Sales subsistence as an unconventional path.
The stuff you wouldn't have thought of: USDA LFPA grants flowing to food banks RIGHT NOW, VA Canteen Service (separate from hospital meals), Job Corps centers, tribal government food programs, government event catering via micro-purchase, and disaster pre-positioning contracts at the STATE level (not just FEMA).
