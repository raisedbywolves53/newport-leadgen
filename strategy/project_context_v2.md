## Project Context

Newport Wholesalers is a $25M-$100M Florida-based grocery distribution and wholesale company with 25+ years of operating history. They have **zero government contract experience** but possess all the operational capabilities needed to serve government buyers (warehouses, trucks, cold chain, broad product range).

The U.S. government food procurement market is $35-50 billion annually. Newport's realistic serviceable market in Florida and the Southeast is $500M-$2B annually.

**Why now:** A historic fraud crackdown has created a "flight to quality" where government buyers are prioritizing verifiable, legitimate American-owned businesses over shell companies and pass-throughs. Newport's 25-year track record, real infrastructure, and American workforce position them perfectly for this moment.

**The client reality:** Newport's owner has built a successful business over three decades and is near retirement. He doesn't need more work — he needs a low-friction path to capitalize on this opportunity. His team knows how to sell and deliver food. They don't know government procurement, and they don't want to learn it.

**Still Mind Creative's role:** Provide the operational infrastructure that makes government contracting possible without Newport hiring staff or changing their workflow. We handle the administrative complexity; their salespeople focus on relationships and closing.

**This is a consulting pitch with a service component.** The proof-of-concept demonstrates the opportunity AND positions Still Mind Creative as the ongoing partner who makes it work.

## Critical Success Factors (From 30-Year Procurement Veteran Perspective)

### 1. Past Performance Is Everything

Government buyers don't trust capability statements. They trust track records. In evaluation criteria, past performance typically carries 40-50% of the weight — not 10%.

**Year 1 is about collecting receipts, not revenue.**

Newport needs to:
- Target micro-purchase opportunities (under $10K) where they can win without past performance
- Document every successful delivery with timestamps, photos, sign-offs
- Request written references after EVERY delivery, no matter how small
- Build a "past performance portfolio" that attaches to future proposals

**Still Mind Creative's role:** 
- Create delivery documentation templates
- Capture and organize performance records
- Build the past performance portfolio as a living document
- Track which agencies have provided references

**Success metric:** 5+ documented past performance references in Year 1 (more important than revenue)

### 2. Relationships Beat Cold Outreach

Government buyers ignore cold emails. They respond to:
- Vendors they've met at industry conferences
- Vendors who responded thoughtfully to Sources Sought notices
- Vendors introduced by trusted colleagues
- Vendors who showed up, asked good questions, and followed up

**Still Mind Creative's role:**
- Identify conferences and register Newport's salesperson (ACFSA, FSNA, ALA)
- Prepare Sources Sought responses that get Newport on agency radars
- Research decision makers and provide briefing packets before meetings
- Draft follow-up communications after facility visits

**Newport's role:** Show up. Build the relationship. Be the face.

### 3. Pre-Solicitation Engagement Wins Contracts

The best vendors shape solicitations before they're published. When an agency publishes a Sources Sought notice, they're asking: "Who can do this? What should we require?"

Vendors who engage during this period:
- Get their capabilities considered when requirements are written
- Build relationships with food service administrators
- Understand agency needs before competitors do

**Still Mind Creative's role:**
- Monitor Sources Sought notices daily
- Draft responses within 7 days of posting
- Identify Industry Day events and register Newport
- Prepare talking points for pre-solicitation meetings

### 4. Incumbent Displacement Requires Targeting

Incumbents with good performance don't get displaced. Newport only wins when:
- Contracts expire with no remaining option years
- Incumbents fail badly enough to trigger termination
- New requirements emerge that incumbents can't serve
- Set-asides exclude the incumbent

**Still Mind Creative's role:**
- Track contract expiration dates 12-18 months out
- Monitor news coverage of food service failures (Aramark lawsuits, Trinity terminations)
- File FOIA/Sunshine Law requests for vendor performance evaluations
- Identify agencies that recently terminated vendors (actively looking for alternatives)

### 5. Legitimacy Must Be Provable

In the post-fraud-crackdown environment, agencies want proof vendors are real. Newport's 25-year history is the moat — but it needs to be packaged for easy verification.

**Still Mind Creative's role:**
Create a "Legitimacy Package" that attaches to every proposal:
- Photos of warehouse facilities (with address visible)
- Photos of truck fleet (with Newport branding)
- Copy of Florida business registration showing founding date
- Bank reference letter confirming long-term relationship
- Sample references from institutional customers
- SQF/HACCP certification copies
- Capability statement with NAICS codes, UEI, CAGE code

### 6. Emergency Response Is a Backdoor

Florida gets hurricanes. When disasters hit, FEMA and state emergency management need food NOW. Emergency contracts are often sole-source or limited competition — speed matters more than price.

**Still Mind Creative's role:**
- Register Newport with FEMA Vendor Profile System
- Register with Florida Division of Emergency Management
- Develop emergency response capability statement
- Pre-position relationships with emergency management contacts
- Maintain "rapid response" protocol for disaster procurement
