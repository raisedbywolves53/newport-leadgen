# Government Decision Maker Contact Sourcing Playbook

**Prepared:** February 2026
**For:** Newport Wholesalers Lead Generation Pipeline

---

## Overview

This playbook documents how to identify and obtain contact information for government food procurement decision makers across federal, state, and local levels. Government contacts are harder to source than private-sector contacts — Apollo.io coverage is weaker, email formats differ, and many government employees don't maintain LinkedIn profiles. A multi-source approach is essential.

---

## Source-by-Source Assessment

### 1. Apollo.io (Segments C & D)

**Best for:** Named individuals with email + phone at government-adjacent organizations (FSMCs, private prisons, commissary companies, GPOs)
**Weaker for:** Direct government employees (federal/state/local agency staff)

**Coverage Assessment:**
- **Private corrections companies** (CoreCivic, GEO Group, Aramark Corrections, Trinity, Keefe): Good coverage. Apollo tracks these as corporate entities with standard email formats
- **Food Service Management Companies** (Aramark, Compass, Sodexo): Good coverage. Large companies with well-indexed employee data
- **Federal agencies** (BOP, VA, USDA, DLA): Moderate coverage. Apollo indexes some federal employees but coverage is patchy — many government email addresses (@bop.gov, @va.gov, @usda.gov) are not in Apollo's database
- **State agencies** (state DOCs, education departments): Weak coverage. State government employees are poorly represented in Apollo
- **Local government** (school districts, county jails): Weak to moderate. Larger districts (Miami-Dade, Broward) may have some coverage; smaller counties will have almost none

**Recommended Apollo Workflow:**
1. Run Segment D (Corrections) first — highest Apollo hit rate because it targets private companies
2. Run Segment C (Government) — expect lower results; focus on filtering for food service and procurement titles
3. Use Apollo results as a starting point, then cross-reference with other sources below

**Expected Hit Rates:**
- Private corrections/FSMC companies: 40–60% of target titles found with email
- Federal agencies: 15–25% of target titles found
- State/local agencies: 5–15% of target titles found

**Cost:**
- People search: Free (0 credits)
- Email reveal: 1 credit per contact ($0.03–$0.10 depending on plan)
- Projected cost for 500 validated government contacts: $15–$50 in Apollo credits + manual sourcing time

### 2. SAM.gov Entity Search

**Best for:** Registered government entities and their Point of Contact (POC) information
**URL:** https://sam.gov/search

**What you get:**
- Entity name, UEI (Unique Entity ID), CAGE Code
- Physical address
- Government Business POC (name, phone, email)
- Electronic Business POC
- Entity type, NAICS codes registered under
- Socioeconomic status (small business, HUBZone, etc.)

**How to use it:**
1. Search by NAICS code (424410, 424490, 722310) + entity type "Government"
2. Filter by state (Florida) for local targeting
3. Export entity listings with POC information
4. POC contacts are typically procurement/contracting office staff — the people who manage vendor relationships

**Coverage:** All federal entities that issue contracts. State/local coverage is partial (only entities registered in SAM.gov, which is required for federal grants/contracts but not state/local)

**Limitations:**
- POC information may be a generic office contact rather than a named individual
- Data may be outdated (entities update SAM.gov annually, not in real-time)
- Does not include food service administrators or operational staff — primarily procurement/contracting contacts

**Cost:** Free

**Expected yield:** 50–200 federal entity POCs relevant to food procurement in Florida

### 3. FPDS (Federal Procurement Data System) — Contract Award Data

**Best for:** Identifying the specific Contracting Officers who signed food procurement contracts
**URL:** https://www.fpds.gov

**What you get:**
- Contract award details (vendor, agency, value, period of performance)
- **Contracting Officer name** — the person who signed the award
- Contracting office location and code
- NAICS and PSC codes
- Competition level (full and open, set-aside, sole source)

**How to use it:**
1. Search FPDS by NAICS codes (424410, 424490, 722310) + state (Florida)
2. Filter by awarding agency (BOP, VA, USDA, DLA, etc.)
3. Export results — includes Contracting Officer names
4. Cross-reference Contracting Officers with agency directories and LinkedIn to get current contact information

**Why this is powerful:**
- Contracting Officers who have awarded food contracts in the past are the most likely to award future food contracts
- Their name on a past award means they have the warrant authority and the food procurement experience
- This is the most direct path to "who actually signs food contracts at this agency in Florida"

**Coverage:** All federal contract awards above the micro-purchase threshold ($10K)

**Limitations:**
- Only federal contracts (no state/local)
- Contracting Officer names may have rotated since the award — verify current assignments
- Does not include phone numbers or emails — need to cross-reference

**Cost:** Free

**Expected yield:** 20–50 named Contracting Officers responsible for food procurement in Florida federal agencies

### 4. Agency Staff Directories and Organizational Charts

**Best for:** Full organizational structure with names, titles, phone numbers, and sometimes emails

**Federal Agencies:**
- **BOP:** Each institution publishes leadership staff on bop.gov. Wardens, Associate Wardens, and department heads (including Food Service Administrator) are typically listed. Call the main institution number and ask for the Food Service department
- **VA:** VA medical center leadership and department contacts are published on va.gov. Nutrition & Food Service (NFS) managers are listed on facility pages
- **USDA:** AMS and FNS organizational charts are published on usda.gov. Procurement staff in the AMS Commodity Procurement division are identifiable
- **DLA:** DLA Troop Support organizational structure is published on dla.mil. Subsistence supply chain contacts are identifiable
- **GSA:** GSA employee directory (gsaelibrary.gsa.gov) includes procurement contacts by category

**State of Florida:**
- **Florida DOC:** Facility superintendent and key staff listed on dc.state.fl.us. Food service contract manager identifiable through DOC central office
- **Florida DOE:** School nutrition staff listed on fldoe.org/schools/nutrition
- **MFMP:** MyFloridaMarketPlace lists category managers and procurement contacts

**Local (Florida):**
- **School Districts:** Each district website has a Food Service / School Nutrition department page with director names and contact info. These are the highest-value targets for Newport
- **County Purchasing:** Each Florida county has a purchasing department with published contact information. County websites list procurement contacts
- **County Jails:** Contact information through county sheriff's office or corrections department

**How to use it:**
1. Start with BOP institutions in Florida — find Food Service Administrator names
2. Compile FL VA medical center NFS manager names
3. Build a list of FL school district Food Service Directors from district websites
4. Identify FL county purchasing contacts from county government websites

**Coverage:** Excellent for federal agencies and larger state/local entities. Smaller counties and municipalities may not publish detailed org charts

**Cost:** Free

**Expected yield:** 30–80 named food service and procurement contacts across FL government entities

### 5. LinkedIn

**Best for:** Individual profiles with job history, current role, and networking capability
**Cost:** Free for basic search; LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/month) for InMail and advanced filters

**Search strategies for government food service roles:**
```
"Food Service Director" AND ("Bureau of Prisons" OR "BOP" OR "Federal Prison")
"Food Service Administrator" AND ("correctional" OR "corrections" OR "prison")
"School Nutrition Director" AND "Florida"
"Food Service Manager" AND ("VA" OR "Veterans Affairs" OR "veteran")
"Contracting Officer" AND ("food" OR "subsistence" OR "grocery")
"Procurement Specialist" AND ("food service" OR "food supply") AND "Florida"
"Dietitian" AND ("corrections" OR "BOP" OR "VA") AND "Florida"
```

**How to use it:**
1. Run targeted searches using the strings above
2. Build a prospect list of names and titles
3. Cross-reference with agency directories to confirm current role
4. Use LinkedIn InMail (Sales Navigator) or find email via Apollo.io/Hunter.io

**Coverage:** Moderate for government food service roles. Many government employees have LinkedIn profiles, but fewer than private-sector equivalents. Younger and mid-career professionals are better represented than senior staff

**Limitations:**
- Government employees may not list their specific agency or facility
- Some agencies discourage LinkedIn use for security roles
- InMail response rates for government employees are typically 5–15%

**Expected yield:** 50–150 relevant profiles for FL government food service/procurement roles

### 6. State Procurement Portal Vendor/Contact Directories

**Best for:** State agency buyer contacts and existing vendor lists

**Florida:**
- **MyFloridaMarketPlace (MFMP):** Lists commodity/category managers who oversee food procurement contracts. Also shows existing food supply contract holders — their renewal dates indicate rebid opportunities
- **Florida FACTS:** Shows contract award information including buyer contacts
- **Florida DOC Vendor Portal:** Lists approved vendors and procurement contacts for corrections
- **Florida DOE School Nutrition:** Lists state-level contacts for the school meal program

**Other Southeast States (for expansion):**
- **Georgia:** Georgia Procurement Registry (doas.ga.gov/state-purchasing)
- **Alabama:** Alabama Purchasing Division (purchasing.alabama.gov)
- **South Carolina:** SC Procurement Services (procurement.sc.gov)
- **North Carolina:** NC eProcurement (eprocurement.nc.gov)

**How to use it:**
1. Register on MFMP (required anyway for state contract bids)
2. Search for active food supply contracts — identify category managers
3. Note contract expiration dates for targeting pre-solicitation outreach
4. Identify existing food vendors as potential competitors or partners

**Coverage:** Good for state-level procurement contacts. Does not include operational staff (food service administrators at individual facilities)

**Cost:** MFMP registration $50/year; other state portals are free

**Expected yield:** 10–30 state procurement contacts across FL and SE states

### 7. Industry Association Directories

**Best for:** Food service professionals who are active in professional development — these are often the most engaged and influential decision makers

**Key Associations:**

| Association | Directory Access | Annual Event | Members Relevant to Newport |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ACFSA** (American Correctional Food Service Association) | Membership directory available to members | Annual conference (August) | Corrections food service directors, state DOC food service chiefs |
| **SNA** (School Nutrition Association) | State chapter directories, ANC expo | Annual National Conference (July), state events | School nutrition directors, food service directors |
| **FSNA** (Florida School Nutrition Association) | Florida chapter of SNA | Annual conference | FL school district food service leaders |
| **ALA** (American Logistics Association) | Member directory, military buyer database | Annual conference + EXPO (January) | Military commissary/exchange buyers, DLA personnel |
| **ANFP** (Association of Nutrition & Foodservice Professionals) | Certified Dietary Manager directory | Annual conference | Healthcare and corrections food service managers |
| **NACUFS** (Nat'l Association of College & University Food Services) | Regional directories | National conference | University dining services directors |

**How to use it:**
1. Join ACFSA ($150/year) and SNA/FSNA ($75/year) immediately — these are the two highest-value associations
2. Access membership directories for contact information
3. Attend conferences — this is the #1 networking opportunity. Conference exhibit halls allow direct conversations with decision makers
4. Join ALA ($500/year) when ready to pursue military opportunities

**Coverage:** Excellent for engaged food service professionals. Bias toward operational/food service roles (vs. procurement/contracting)

**Cost:** $75–$500/year per association, plus conference attendance ($500–$2,000 per event including travel)

**Expected yield:** 20–50 high-quality named contacts per association, with opportunity for in-person relationship building

### 8. USDA AMS Qualified Bidders List

**Best for:** USDA AMS procurement contacts and understanding the commodity procurement process

**What you get:**
- List of all vendors approved to bid on USDA AMS commodity solicitations
- AMS procurement office contacts (Commodity Procurement Branch staff)
- Solicitation schedule and upcoming purchase cycles

**How to use it:**
1. Review the Qualified Bidders List to understand who the current approved vendors are (competitive intelligence)
2. Contact AMS Commodity Procurement Branch staff to discuss the vendor approval process
3. Identify AMS personnel who manage food commodity categories relevant to Newport

**Coverage:** AMS-specific only

**Cost:** Free

**Expected yield:** 5–10 AMS procurement contacts, plus competitive intelligence on approved vendor landscape

### 9. Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Requests

**Best for:** Obtaining specific contract documents, vendor lists, pricing, and performance evaluations that aren't publicly available

**When to use FOIA:**
- Request a copy of the current food supply contract at a specific FL BOP institution (to understand incumbent pricing and terms)
- Request the vendor performance evaluation records for food supply contracts (to identify dissatisfied agencies)
- Request the list of approved vendors for food supply at a state agency
- Request the solicitation evaluation scoring sheets from a past food procurement (to understand how bids are scored)

**How to file:**
- Federal: Submit through the agency's FOIA portal (every federal agency has one)
- Florida: Use Florida's Sunshine Law (Chapter 119, Florida Statutes) — broader than federal FOIA. Submit public records requests to the specific agency
- Typical response time: 20 business days (federal), 2–7 days (Florida Sunshine Law — much faster)

**Cost:** Free to file. Agencies may charge for copying/processing if the request is voluminous

**Expected yield:** Specific contract details, pricing benchmarks, vendor performance data — invaluable for competitive positioning

## Data Quality and Validation

### Validation Steps for Each Contact

1. **Verify name and title** — Cross-reference across at least two sources (e.g., Apollo + LinkedIn, or FPDS + agency directory)
2. **Verify current employment** — Government employees transfer frequently. Confirm the person still holds the role via agency website or phone call
3. **Verify email** — Government email formats are predictable (@bop.gov, @va.gov, @usda.gov, @fldoe.org, @[county].fl.us). Verify format before sending outreach
4. **Verify decision authority** — Confirm the contact has procurement or specification influence (not just an operational role without purchasing input)
5. **Note best contact method** — Some government employees prefer phone calls over email due to strict email management policies. Note preferred contact method when discovered

### Government Email Formats

| Agency / Level | Email Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Federal (BOP) | firstname.lastname@bop.gov | john.smith@bop.gov |
| Federal (VA) | firstname.lastname@va.gov | jane.doe@va.gov |
| Federal (USDA) | firstname.lastname@usda.gov | bob.jones@usda.gov |
| Federal (DLA) | firstname.lastname@dla.mil | — |
| Florida State | firstname.lastname@fldoc.com (DOC) | — |
| FL School Districts | Varies by district | firstname.lastname@browardschools.com |
| FL Counties | Varies by county | firstname.lastname@miamidade.gov |

### CRM Tagging

When entering government contacts into the pipeline, tag with:
- **Source** (Apollo, FPDS, LinkedIn, agency directory, conference, etc.)
- **Level** (federal, state, local)
- **Agency/Entity** (BOP, VA, specific school district, etc.)
- **Role type** (contracting officer, food service administrator, nutrition director, procurement specialist)
- **Decision authority** (signs contracts, writes specs, influences selection, operational only)
- **Validation status** (verified, unverified, stale)
- **Last verified date**
