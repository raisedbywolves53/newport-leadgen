## Problem Statement

**Who has this problem:** Newport Wholesalers is a 30-year, American-owned grocery wholesaler based in Plantation, FL (Broward County). They distribute confectionery, snacks, shelf-stable food, and dry goods across South Florida from their own warehouse and delivery fleet. Revenue is estimated at $25M–$100M. The business is profitable, well-run, and has virtually zero employee turnover.

**What the pain point is:** Newport's revenue is concentrated in a single sales channel — commercial wholesale. They have no presence in government procurement (a $7.17B federal food market with an additional $600M–$1.2B in Florida state/local spending). They also lack a systematic outbound prospecting engine for their core commercial business. Both represent significant untapped revenue that compounds over time.

**How they solve it today:** They don't. Government contracting is an entirely new channel with zero past performance. Commercial sales growth is relationship-driven and manual — no structured SDR process, no enrichment pipeline, no automated outreach.

**Why the timing is exceptional:** The post-DOGE/fraud-crackdown environment is fundamentally shifting how government agencies evaluate vendors. Agencies face increasing public scrutiny over contracting decisions. Shell companies, paper-only vendors, and contractors with questionable histories are being exposed and excluded. Newport — American-owned by a citizen born of legal immigrants, 30 years of continuous operations, real warehouse infrastructure, W-2 American workforce, clean audit history, full tax compliance — is exactly the type of vendor agencies need going forward. This competitive moat took three decades to build and cannot be manufactured overnight.

## Solution Overview

Still Mind Creative LLC is building a comprehensive growth strategy and operational support system for Newport across two parallel channels.

**Channel 1 — Government Contracting:** A strategic 5-year plan for entering federal, state, and local food procurement. The approach is phased: Year 1 focuses on winning micro-purchase contracts (under $15K) that require minimal proposal effort and build past-performance credibility. As credibility accumulates, Newport graduates to simplified acquisition contracts ($15K–$350K) and SLED (state/local/education) opportunities. The compound effect of contract renewals (70% incumbent renewal rate) creates a growing base of recurring revenue. Still Mind handles all monitoring, bid preparation, compliance, and administrative tasks — Newport focuses exclusively on business relationships and delivery.

**Channel 2 — Commercial SDR Agent:** An AI-powered outbound prospecting system for Newport's core wholesale business. The system identifies ideal customer profile (ICP) buyers and suppliers, enriches contact data, and creates personalized outreach sequences. This runs in parallel with the government channel, generates faster and larger wins because it extends Newport's existing business, and requires Newport's input primarily on value proposition and messaging.

**What we deliver to Newport ownership:** Two presentation decks (one per channel) that tell a compelling, research-backed story — not a sales pitch, but a professional strategic assessment. Each deck is supplemented by an interactive financial model (Excel) where Newport can adjust their own inputs (COGS, margins, delivery radius, certifications) to see how scenarios change. The decks sell the vision; the spreadsheets prove the economics.

## Target Users

### Primary: Newport Ownership (Decision Makers)

Newport's ownership evaluates business opportunities visually. They prefer clean charts, tables, and graphs over dense text. They think in terms of practical outcomes: What does this cost? What does it return? What do I have to do differently? They are conservative decision-makers who value credibility and defensibility over hype.

- **What they need to see:** A clear path from today to $X in government revenue, backed by real data, with honest acknowledgment of what we don't know yet (and what we need from them to refine projections)
- **What they DON'T want:** Cheesy pipedream projections, hard sells, or assumptions about their business that we haven't validated
- **How they consume information:** Visual-first (charts and tables), supplemented by short-sentence text (Buffett-style: direct, no fluff)

### Secondary: Newport Operations Staff

The people who would actually fulfill government contracts — warehouse managers, delivery drivers, accounting staff. They don't see the decks, but the strategy must be realistic given their operational capacity.

### Tertiary: Still Mind (Us)

We are both the strategists and the operators. The documentation and systems we build must enable us to run the day-to-day: monitoring opportunities, preparing bids, managing compliance, tracking the pipeline. We don't charge Newport a consulting fee — we prove the model works using their business, then extract a repeatable playbook.

### Who This Is NOT For

- Investors or lenders (this is an internal strategic decision, not a fundraising pitch)
- Newport's existing commercial customers (they don't need to know about the government channel)
- Other consulting firms (this is proprietary methodology, not a case study — yet)

## Success Criteria

### GovCon Channel

**Quantitative (Year 1):**
- Newport is registered on SAM.gov, MFMP, and at least 5 county/school district portals
- 40–80+ bids submitted across micro-purchase and simplified acquisition tiers
- 8–15 contract wins (primarily micro-purchases for credibility building)
- $50K–$150K in government contract revenue
- Positive owner earnings by end of Year 2 (Year 1 is a deliberate investment)

**Quantitative (5-Year):**
- Active contract base of 50–70 contracts with 70% renewal rate
- Annual revenue trajectory toward $3M+ by Year 5
- Portfolio shift: micro-purchases phase out, replaced by simplified and sealed-bid contracts
- Cumulative owner earnings of $500K–$840K over 5 years (paid route)

**Qualitative:**
- Newport ownership understands the two-routes decision (free vs. paid tools) and its impact
- Newport can answer the 10 Key Questions that determine every number in the model
- Newport views government contracting as a long-term competitive moat, not a short-term revenue play
- Newport's 30-year track record is positioned as a strategic asset in every bid

### Commercial SDR Channel

**Quantitative:**
- ICP buyer and supplier lists sourced and validated across 5 segments
- Contact data enriched with accurate email/phone for 70%+ of targets
- Outreach sequences generating qualified meetings for Newport sales team
- Measurable pipeline: X leads sourced → Y meetings booked → Z deals closed

**Qualitative:**
- Newport sales team has a consistent flow of qualified prospects without manual research
- Outreach messaging reflects Newport's actual value proposition (not generic templates)
- System can run on autopilot with minimal ongoing intervention

### Proposal Deliverables

**The decks are successful when:**
- Newport ownership makes a go/no-go decision within 2 weeks of presentation
- They can articulate the strategy back to us in their own words
- They fill in the Key Questions section of the financial model without prompting
- They don't ask "where did you get these numbers?" — because the data sources are cited inline

**The financial models are successful when:**
- Newport adjusts the input variables and the model doesn't break
- The three scenarios (conservative/moderate/aggressive) feel realistic, not optimistic
- Newport trusts the model enough to use it for their own internal planning

### Known Assumptions

1. Newport's gross margin on government contracts will be approximately 18–25% depending on contract tier (needs validation from ownership)
2. Newport can profitably fulfill orders under $5,200 (the micro-purchase threshold — critical question for ownership)
3. Newport's delivery radius covers at minimum South Florida, potentially Southeast US (needs validation)
4. Newport has or can obtain food safety certifications (SQF/GFSI/FDA) — largest cost variable if not
5. Win rates for micro-purchases are 15–25% for a new entrant with no past performance, growing to 25%+ as credibility builds
6. 70% of government food contracts renew to the incumbent vendor — this is the compounding flywheel
7. The post-DOGE/fraud-crackdown environment will increase demand for transparent, auditable vendors like Newport — this is a thesis, not a certainty, but supported by current policy direction
