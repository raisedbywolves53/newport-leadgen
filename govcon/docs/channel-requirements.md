# Channel Requirements & Credentials — Validated Feb 28, 2026

## Tier 1: Federal Micro-Purchase (<$15K) — LOWEST BARRIER

### Requirements
- **SAM.gov**: Recommended but technically not required (many done via GPC/phone)
- **UEI**: Obtained through SAM.gov registration
- **Certifications**: None required
- **Insurance**: None required
- **Bonding**: None
- **Past performance**: Not evaluated
- **Process**: Quote by phone/email, purchase card (GPC) transaction, or GSA Advantage
- **Evaluation**: Lowest price, no formal source selection

### Timeline to Ready
- 3-6 weeks (SAM.gov registration processing)

### Newport Status
- Can start almost immediately
- 35-year FL operations = instant credibility for phone/email quotes
- GovSpend reveals these invisible transactions (not on SAM.gov)

### Key Facts
- 83% of federal food contracts are micro-purchases (FPDS FY2024)
- 93% sole source for NAICS 424490 @ DoD
- Invisible on SAM.gov — requires GovSpend to discover
- Government Purchase Card limit: $10,000 standard, up to $25,000 for certain categories

## Tier 2: Federal Simplified Acquisition ($15K-$350K) — LOW-MODERATE BARRIER

### Requirements
- **SAM.gov**: Required (active, current registration)
- **Permits**: FDA food facility registration + FDACS state food permit
- **Insurance**: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate CGL (commercial general liability)
- **Bonding**: Not typically required below $150K; bid bond possible for $150K-$350K range
- **Past performance**: NOT required — FAR 15.305(a)(2) assigns "neutral" rating to new entrants (explicitly not negative)
- **Evaluation method**: Mostly LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable) — price wins
- **Set-aside rules**: Automatically set aside for small business if "rule of two" met (2+ SB expected to bid)
- **Capability statement**: 1-2 page summary of company qualifications

### SBA Certifications (Optional but Valuable)
- **8(a)**: Sole-source up to $4.5M ($7M for manufacturing)
- **HUBZone**: 10% price evaluation preference, sole-source up to $4.5M
- **SDVOSB**: Sole-source up to $4.5M
- **WOSB**: Sole-source up to $4.5M (certain NAICS)
- **SDB**: Automatic via SAM.gov self-certification

### Timeline
- 4-8 months to first win (including registration + first bid cycles)

### Newport Status
- FDA + FDACS likely already held (35yr food distributor)
- Needs SAM.gov registration + insurance verification
- Neutral past performance means no disadvantage as new entrant
- Price-competitive with existing distribution infrastructure

### Key Facts
- FAR Part 13 governs simplified acquisition
- Combined synopsis/solicitation is the standard format
- Contracting officers have streamlined authority — faster decisions
- Most food/grocery contracts are LPTA (price is primary differentiator)

## Tier 3: FL SLED School Districts — MODERATE BARRIER

### Requirements
- **E-Verify**: Mandatory for all FL public contracts
- **Vendor registration**: Per-district (67 districts, each has own portal/process)
- **Permits**: FDACS food distribution permit
- **Insurance**: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate CGL (most districts require)
- **Background checks**: Jessica Lunsford Act (FL §1012.465) — Level 2 fingerprint screening for anyone with direct school access
- **USDA compliance**: 2 CFR 200.318-326 (federal funds flow through school meal programs)
- **Buy American**: Required for USDA-funded school nutrition programs
- **Business license**: FL active corporation/LLC
- **References**: Typically 3 comparable food distribution references

### Cooperative Purchasing (Force Multiplier)
- **BuyBoard**: Win one cooperative contract → all 67 FL districts can purchase without additional bidding
- **Florida Buy**: State cooperative via Association of Educational Purchasing Agencies
- **District-to-district piggybacking**: FL law allows — win one large district = cascade to others
- **OMNIA Partners**: National cooperative (US Foods is a vendor)

### Procurement Portals
- MFMP (state-level)
- DemandStar (Broward + largest districts)
- BidNet Direct (Palm Beach + growing)
- VendorLink (some counties)
- FL DOE Bid List
- Individual district procurement pages

### Bid Season
- January-April: RFPs released for next school year
- March-June: Evaluation and awards
- July-August: New contracts begin (aligns with school year)
- Multi-year: Typically 1-year base + 4 renewal options

### Timeline
- 3-6 months from registration to first contract
- Portal registration: 2-4 weeks per district
- Background checks: 2-4 weeks processing

### Newport Status
- 35yr FL ops + warehouse + fleet = strong fit
- Already serves commercial grocery — school district food supply is adjacent
- Needs: portal registrations, E-Verify enrollment, background checks for delivery staff
- Start with small districts (2-3 bidders, $200K-$3M) then scale via cooperatives

### Key Facts
- FL has 67 school districts, 2.87M students, 3rd largest state K-12
- Small districts (<30K students, ~45 districts): 2-3 bidders typical
- Cooperative purchasing can turn 1 win into 20-50 customers
- School food programs run on federal USDA dollars — stable funding stream

## Tier 4: FL Corrections & Jails — MODERATE-HIGH BARRIER

### FL DOC (State Prisons — 81K inmates)
- **Current status**: LOCKED under existing contracts through 2027+
  - Food supply: Cheney Brothers/PFG, Contract C2808 ($678.7M/10yr, ~$68M/yr)
  - Food service mgmt: Aramark, Contract C3021 ($449M/5yr, ~$88M/yr through ~April 2027)
- **Newport path**: Subcontract to Aramark or Cheney Brothers, NOT compete for prime
- **Requirements for sub**: GFSI food safety certification preferred, FL warehouse + fleet, insurance
- **Timeline for DOC rebid**: Monitor for C3021 rebid (2027), C2808 rebid (2028+)

### County Jails (67 counties, 54K inmates)
- **More accessible** than state DOC — each county procures independently
- **MFMP registration**: Required for state/local procurement
- **Security screening**: Background checks for delivery drivers entering jail facilities
- **HACCP**: Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points food safety plan required
- **Bonding**: Required for contracts >$100K-$200K (varies by county)
- **Insurance**: $1M/$2M CGL minimum, some require $5M umbrella
- **References**: 2-3 institutional food supply references preferred

## Tier 5: Federal Large (>$350K) / Set-Aside — HIGH BARRIER

### Requirements (everything from Tier 2, plus:)
- **Past performance**: 3+ relevant contracts required (formally evaluated per FAR 15.305)
- **Bonding**: Bid bond (5% of bid price) + performance bond (100% of contract value) + payment bond (100%)
- **Financial statements**: May require audited financials or bank letter of credit
- **Capability statement**: Detailed, including past performance matrix, org chart, key personnel
- **Quality management**: Documented food safety / quality assurance program
- **Evaluation**: Full source selection (price + technical + past performance + management)

### SBA Certifications (Critical for Set-Asides)
- **8(a)**: Sole-source up to $4.5M, competitive set-aside unlimited
- **HUBZone**: 10% price evaluation preference
- **SDVOSB**: VA sole-source up to $4.5M
- Certification timeline: 8(a) = 90-180 days, HUBZone = 60-90 days

### Timeline
- 12-24 months (need past performance from micro + simplified wins first)
- Build: micro wins → simplified wins → large competitive

### Newport Status
- Not ready Year 1 — no federal past performance
- Year 3-4 target after building track record in micro + simplified
- SBA certifications should be pursued Year 1 (long lead time)

## Tier 6: Subcontracting — VARIABLE BARRIER

### DLA Prime Vendor Subs (LOW barrier)
- **SAM.gov**: Registration required
- **Capability statement**: Company overview + distribution capabilities
- **Insurance**: $1M/$2M CGL
- **How to find primes**: DoD Prime Contractor Directory, SBLOs (Small Business Liaison Officers)
- **Legal requirement**: Primes MUST maintain SB subcontracting plans on contracts >$750K (FAR 52.219-9)
- **FL primes**: East CONUS prime vendor (likely Sysco or US Foods)
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months to establish first relationship
- **Typical sub value**: $250K-$1M/yr per relationship

### FSMC Subs — Aramark/Compass/Sodexo (MODERATE-HIGH barrier)
- **GFSI certification**: Required — SQF, BRC, or FSSC 22000 (**biggest barrier**)
  - SQF certification: 3-6 months, $5K-$15K (audit + consulting)
  - BRC certification: 3-6 months, similar cost
  - FSSC 22000: 4-8 months, $8K-$20K
- **Procurement portals**:
  - Aramark → qualifies through Avendra (GPO)
  - Compass → qualifies through Foodbuy (GPO)
  - Sodexo → direct supplier portal
- **Vendor onboarding**: 60-120 days per FSMC after certification
- **Timeline**: 6-12 months (GFSI certification is rate-limiting step)
- **Typical sub value**: $500K-$5M/yr per FSMC

### USDA Commodity Distribution (LOW-MODERATE barrier)
- **FDACS vendor registration**: Required (FL Dept of Agriculture and Consumer Services)
- **Programs**: TEFAP, LFPA (Local Food Purchase Assistance), Farm to School
- **LFPA**: Explicitly designed for local/regional distributors — Newport is ideal
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months
- **Typical value**: $200K-$2M per bid, 3-5 bids/year

### SBA Mentor-Protege Program (HIGH setup, HIGH reward)
- **Application**: ~105 days for SBA approval
- **Protege requirements**: Small business, operating 1+ year, good character
- **Mentor provides**: Technical assistance, management guidance, financial backstop, past performance sharing
- **Joint Venture (JV)**: Protege must perform 40% of work
- **Benefits**: JV can bid on SB set-asides using mentor's past performance
- **Newport fit**: Ideal protege — 35yr FL ops, warehouse/fleet, W-2 workforce, zero federal past performance
- **DoD Mentor-Protege**: Separate program, mentor gets reimbursement for assistance costs
- **Timeline**: 6-12 months to establish, Year 3+ for meaningful revenue
- **Potential**: $1M-$5M/yr through JV set-aside contracts

## Critical Path Items (Start Immediately)
1. **SAM.gov registration** — unlocks all federal (3-6 weeks)
2. **SBA size standard verification** — confirm small business status for NAICS 424490
3. **E-Verify enrollment** — required for all FL public contracts
4. **FDACS vendor registration** — unlocks USDA programs + school districts
5. **Insurance review** — verify $1M/$2M CGL coverage
6. **Capability statement** — 1-2 page company overview for primes + agencies
7. **SBA certification applications** — 8(a), HUBZone (long lead time, high value)
8. **GFSI certification planning** — rate-limiting step for FSMC sub opportunities
