# Win Rates, Progression & Portfolio Strategy — Validated Feb 28, 2026

## Key Finding: LPTA Favors New Entrants in Food Distribution

Most federal food contracts use **LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable)** evaluation:
- All non-price factors (including past performance) are **pass/fail only**
- A "neutral" past performance rating (new entrant) PASSES — treated identically to "Exceptional" CPARS
- **Lowest price wins** after technical acceptability is met
- Food distribution is commodity procurement — agencies know what they need
- Source: FAR Part 15, DAU Acquipedia, CRS Report IF10968

**Implication:** Newport's 35-year distribution infrastructure + competitive wholesale pricing is the primary differentiator, not federal past performance.

## Past Performance Accumulation Timeline

### CPARS Thresholds (FAR 42.1502)
- **Micro-purchases (<$15K):** NO CPARS entry. Performance is "invisible" to other agencies.
- **Simplified (<$350K):** NO mandatory CPARS. COs may voluntarily enter but rarely do.
- **Above $350K:** MANDATORY annual + completion CPARS evaluation.

### How to Cite Performance Without CPARS
- Self-reported references in proposals: contract number, agency, CO name, phone, period, value, description
- CO can verify by calling references
- Citable **immediately upon successful delivery** — no minimum duration, no completion certificate, no waiting period
- Valid for 3 years from delivery date (FAR 42.1503)

### Commercial Experience Is Citable (FAR 15.305)
FAR 15.305(a)(2)(ii) requires solicitations to provide offerors opportunity to identify past or current contracts **"including Federal, State, and local government and private"** for similar efforts.

**Newport's 35 years of FL commercial food distribution IS citable from Day 1.** Frame as:
- Scale: 35 years, $25-100M annual distribution volume
- Capability match: Cold chain, multi-stop delivery, perishable inventory management
- Geographic relevance: FL-based warehouse + fleet
- Reliability: Decades continuous operations, auditable history, W-2 workforce

### SLED Contracts Count as Federal Past Performance
FAR 15.305(a)(2)(ii) explicitly includes state and local government contracts. A $2M school district contract is worth more than twenty $8K micro-purchases for credibility on federal bids.

### Subcontract Experience Is Citable
- FAR 15.305: Agencies "should" consider subcontractor PP for major/critical aspects
- 13 CFR 125.2(g): Agencies **must** consider sub PP when: (1) prime is small business, (2) prime lacks independent PP, (3) sub is first-tier, (4) sub is small business
- Newport should proactively request CPARS ratings from prime contractors (federal register rule July 2022 enables this)

## SBA Certification Strategy

### 8(a) — DEPRIORITIZE
- Trump SBA accepted only **65 new 8(a) firms in all of 2025** (down from 2,100+ under Biden)
- **97% reduction** in acceptance rate
- 1,091 existing firms suspended, 154 DC firms being terminated
- Race-based presumption of social disadvantage eliminated (Jan 22, 2026 guidance)
- All applicants must demonstrate individual social disadvantage through fact-specific inquiry
- **Not a realistic near-term path** unless owner has specific, documentable social disadvantage
- Source: SBA Jan 22, 2026 guidance, SmallGovCon, Holland & Knight analysis

### HUBZone — CHECK IMMEDIATELY
- 10% price evaluation preference on LPTA = devastating in thin-margin food distribution
- Sole-source authority up to $5.5M (updated Oct 2025)
- **Plantation, FL likely does NOT qualify** (middle-class suburban, median HH income $65-75K)
- Adjacent Broward areas (Lauderhill, North Lauderdale, parts of Fort Lauderdale) may have qualifying census tracts
- **Action:** Check exact address at maps.certify.sba.gov/hubzone/map (2-minute lookup)
- If not in HUBZone: could establish satellite office in qualifying tract OR qualify via 35% employee residency
- Certification timeline: 3-5 months

### SBA Mentor-Protege — BEST ACCELERATOR FOR LARGE CONTRACTS
- **No socioeconomic certification required** — any SBA-certified small business can be protege
- JV can use mentor's past performance (January 2025 final rule)
- JV retains protege's small business status for set-aside bidding
- Protege must perform 40% of JV work
- **Unlimited contracts** within 2-year window from first award (replaced prior 3-contract limit)
- Newport is ideal protege: 35yr ops, warehouse/fleet, W-2 workforce, zero federal PP
- Timeline: ~105 days SBA approval + 3-6 months to first bid = 9-20 months to first JV contract
- Potential: $200K-$5M contracts in Year 2-3

### Plain Small Business — AUTOMATIC
- Newport qualifies automatically (under $200M revenue threshold for NAICS 424490)
- 23% of federal spending is set aside for small business ($183B in FY2024)
- Can self-certify as Small Disadvantaged Business (SDB) if ownership meets criteria
- No application needed — SAM.gov registration includes size determination

## Revenue Growth Shape: S-Curve

### Phase 1: Foundation (Year 1)
- Revenue growth slow, potentially net negative after costs
- Limited to micro-purchases + small simplified + first school districts
- Win rates low (10-20% blended for competitive bids)
- No CPARS entries yet (below $350K)
- No renewals yet (contracts haven't completed first cycle)
- **Micro-purchases are loss leaders** — buying track record, not making money

### Phase 2: Acceleration (Year 2-4)
- Renewals begin (70% of Y1 contracts renew, creating base revenue)
- Past performance enables larger contracts (simplified + SLED)
- Win rates improve: 25-35% Y2, 35-40% Y3
- Average contract value grows as portfolio shifts micro → simplified → SLED
- First CPARS entries generate formal, searchable record
- **Revenue can 2-3x year-over-year** in this phase

### Phase 3: Maturation (Year 5+)
- Growth rate decelerates but absolute revenue is substantial
- Renewal base provides stability (70%+ incumbent win rate)
- New wins are incremental additions to large base
- Set-asides, Mentor-Protege JV, and FSMC subs driving growth
- Revenue growth rate: 15-25% YoY (down from 80-100% in Phase 2)

### Compounding Flywheel (5 Self-Reinforcing Loops)
1. **Past Performance Loop:** Wins → PP refs → higher win rates → more wins
2. **Renewal Loop:** Completed contracts renew at 70%+ → zero bid cost → fund new pursuits
3. **Contract Value Escalation:** Track record → larger bids → same wins = more revenue
4. **Portfolio Diversification:** Micro → simplified → SLED → set-asides → large
5. **Relationship Loop:** Repeat deliveries → buyer familiarity → directed awards → less competition

## Minimum Time in Loss Leader Phase — Strategy

The fastest path through unprofitable micro-purchases:

1. **Run all accessible channels in parallel from Day 1** — micro + school districts + county jails simultaneously. Don't do micro sequentially before SLED.
2. **SLED wins count as federal PP** — a $2M school district contract > twenty $8K micro-purchases for credibility
3. **HUBZone (if eligible) = skip the line** — 10% price preference on LPTA in thin-margin food = near-guaranteed wins
4. **Mentor-Protege JV = skip to large** — use mentor's PP to bid $350K-$5M in Year 2-3
5. **Commercial experience citable from Day 1** — Newport isn't starting from zero
6. **Cooperative contracts (BuyBoard) = force multiplier** — win one, access 67 districts
7. **Target low-competition categories first** — NAICS 424490 @ DoD (1.2 bidders), confectionery 424450 (1.6 bidders, 58% sole source)
