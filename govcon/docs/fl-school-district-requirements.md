## 1. Registrations Required

**Confidence: HIGH** — statutory requirements

### A. E-Verify Registration (MANDATORY)

- **Authority**: Florida Labor Statute XXXI (effective Jan 1, 2021)
- **Requirement**: ALL public employers, contractors, and subcontractors must register with and use E-Verify to verify work authorization of all newly hired employees
- **No public contract can be entered into without an E-Verify certificate**
- **During vendor registration, districts require upload of E-Verify Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with E-Verify number**
- Source: [Fisher Phillips - FL E-Verify Law](https://www.fisherphillips.com/en/news-insights/floridas-mandatory-e-verify-law.html)

### B. District-Specific Vendor Registration (MANDATORY, per-district)

Each FL school district maintains its own vendor registration system. Registration is typically free. Common platforms:

| District | Registration Platform | Notes |
|----------|----------------------|-------|
| Broward County (BCPS) | eProcure Supplier Portal | DemandStar for bid notifications |
| Miami-Dade (M-DCPS) | Vendor Application FM-3921 | BidSync for solicitations |
| Palm Beach County | BidNet Direct (eff. Jan 2025) | Vendor Compliance Management System |
| Pasco County | IonWave | Electronic vendor application required |
| St. Johns County | District purchasing portal | + Background screening registration |
| Escambia County | District purchasing portal | DemandStar for notifications |

**Required documents for district vendor registration (typical):**
- E-Verify MOU with E-Verify number
- Vendor Affidavit Regarding the Use of Coercion for Labor and Services
- Vendor Affidavit Regarding Companies of Foreign Concern
- W-9 form
- Sunbiz.org registration screen print (active FL business registration)
- Certificate of Insurance
- Source: [St. Johns County Purchasing](https://www.stjohns.k12.fl.us/purchasing/business/)

### C. DemandStar Registration (RECOMMENDED)

- **What**: Web-based electronic procurement system used by many FL school districts for bid notifications
- **Cost**: One free agency subscription; paid plans for multi-agency alerts
- **Used by**: Broward, Miami-Dade, Escambia, and many other FL districts
- **Contact**: 1-800-711-1712

### D. BidNet Direct (RECOMMENDED)

### E. MyFloridaMarketPlace / MFMP (FOR STATE-LEVEL CONTRACTS)

- **Registration**: Through DMS MFMP Vendor Information Portal (VIP)

## 2. Certifications & Licenses

**Confidence: HIGH** — regulatory requirements

### A. FDACS Wholesale/Manufactured Food Establishment Permit (MANDATORY)

- **Authority**: Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services, Division of Food Safety
- **Applies to**: Food manufacturers, processors, storage warehouses, and distributors selling food at wholesale
- **Cold and dry storage warehouses and distribution facilities require this permit**
- **Application timeline**: Submit 21 days before opening/operating
- **Inspection**: Field inspectors contact within 3-5 business days; inspection typically within 2 weeks
- **Renewal**: Annual; late fees up to $100
- **Permits are non-transferable** (new application needed for ownership or location change)
- **Must also register with FDA** as a food facility (all wholesale/manufactured food establishments)
- **Contact**: FoodSafety@FDACS.gov or 1-800-HELP-FLA (435-7352)
- Source: [FDACS Wholesale Food Permit](https://www.fdacs.gov/Business-Services/Food/Food-Establishments/Wholesale-Manufactured-Food-Establishment-Permit)

### B. FDA Food Facility Registration (MANDATORY)

- **All wholesale/manufactured food establishments must register with the U.S. FDA as food facilities**
- Required in addition to FDACS permit
- Source: [FDACS Food Establishments](https://www.fdacs.gov/Business-Services/Food-Establishments)

### C. HACCP-Based Food Safety Program (CONTEXT-DEPENDENT)

- **The National School Lunch Act requires Program Operators (school districts) to have HACCP-based food safety programs**
- Vendors are not directly required to be HACCP-certified, BUT:
  - Districts evaluate vendor food safety capabilities in bids
  - Having a documented HACCP plan or third-party HACCP certification is a strong competitive advantage
  - USDA Approved Supplier Programs (ASP) meet USDA, FDA, and GFSI requirements
  - Districts must monitor vendor compliance with food safety standards per 2 CFR 200.318(b)
- Source: [USDA HACCP Guidance for Schools](https://www.fns.usda.gov/fs/developing-school-food-safety-program-based-process-approach-haccp)

### D. FDACS Vendor Training for NSLP (RECOMMENDED)

- **Online training available** at https://agacademy.fdacs.gov/
- Minimum passing score: 80%
- Covers USDA National School Lunch Program and Summer Food Service Program requirements
- **Required for vendors contracting with Summer Food Service Program sponsors**
- Submit certificate of completion to be added to FDACS vendor list
- **Note**: "Vendors are not required to be on this list to supply their services to the programs" but it helps sponsors identify available providers
- **Contact**: Suzette Bryant, (850) 617-4733, Suzette.Bryant@FDACS.gov
- Source: [FDACS NSLP Vendors](https://www.fdacs.gov/Food-Nutrition/Nutrition-Programs/National-School-Lunch-Program/Vendors)

### E. Optional but Advantageous Certifications

| Certification | Value | Notes |
|--------------|-------|-------|
| SQF (Safe Quality Food) | HIGH | GFSI-benchmarked, shows food safety rigor |
| ServSafe Manager | MEDIUM | Required at facility level in many jurisdictions |
| Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) | MEDIUM | Baseline for wholesale operations |
| Organic/Non-GMO certifications | LOW-MEDIUM | If serving districts with organic meal programs |
| Small/Micro Business Enterprise (SBE/MBE) | MEDIUM | Miami-Dade and other large districts have SBE programs |

## 3. Insurance Requirements

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH** — standard practice across FL districts, specific amounts may vary

### Typical Minimum Insurance Requirements

| Coverage Type | Minimum Per Occurrence | Aggregate | Notes |
|--------------|----------------------|-----------|-------|
| Commercial General Liability | $1,000,000 | $2,000,000 - $3,000,000 | Including Bodily Injury, Property Damage, Product Liability, Completed Operations |
| Automobile Liability | $1,000,000 | — | Including owned, hired, non-owned vehicles |
| Workers' Compensation | FL Statutory Limits | — | Required for all employers with 4+ employees |
| Product Liability | Included in CGL | Included in CGL | Critical for food distributors |
| Umbrella/Excess Liability | $1,000,000 - $5,000,000 | Varies | Large districts (Broward, M-DCPS) may require higher |

### Key Insurance Requirements

- **Insurance provider must be rated A- or better by AM Best**
- **School Board must be named as Additional Insured on all liability policies**
- **Waiver of Subrogation** applies to the school district, its agents, employees, and officials on liability and workers' compensation policies
- **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** must be submitted with bid; if current COI doesn't meet bid requirements, a letter from the carrier confirming they will write the necessary coverage if awarded is acceptable
- **30-day notice of cancellation** to the district is standard
- Sources: [Saint Andrew's School Vendor Requirements](https://www.saintandrews.net/vendors), [St. Johns County ITB](https://www.stjohns.k12.fl.us/purchasing/wp-content/uploads/sites/30/2015/02/2018-07-ITB.pdf)

- Confirm Product Liability is included in CGL policy
- Confirm Automobile Liability covers hired/non-owned vehicles
- Verify AM Best rating of current carrier
- May need to increase umbrella coverage for large district bids

## 4. Bonding Requirements

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — varies significantly by district and contract size

### Performance & Payment Bonds

- **When required**: Typically for contracts above certain dollar thresholds (varies by district)
- **Amount**: 100% of contract amount when required
- **Timeline**: Must be furnished within 10-30 calendar days from date of award, prior to commencement of performance
- **Surety**: Must be issued by a company qualified to do business in the State of Florida
- Source: [Pasco County Schools Purchasing](https://www.pasco.k12.fl.us/purchasing/page/business/)

### Bid Bonds / Bid Deposits

- **When required**: For formal sealed bids (ITBs) at some districts
- **Amount**: Typically 5-10% of bid price (based on FL public contracting norms)
- **Note**: "Bids received without a bid deposit or with a bid deposit in an amount less than required will not be considered"
- **Return**: Returned to unsuccessful bidders immediately following award; returned to successful bidder after signed contract and receipt of performance bond
- Source: [Pasco County Schools](https://www.pasco.k12.fl.us/purchasing/page/business/)

### Key Distinction: Food Supply vs. Construction

- **Bonding requirements are much more common and larger for construction contracts**
- **Food supply contracts often do NOT require bid bonds or performance bonds**, especially for:
  - Smaller districts
  - Contracts under $100K
  - Blanket Purchase Agreements (BPAs) vs. firm fixed-price contracts
- **Larger districts (Broward, Miami-Dade) are more likely to require bonds for major food supply contracts**
- Bond requirements are specified in each individual ITB — always review the specific solicitation

## 5. USDA / Federal Compliance

**Confidence: HIGH** — federal regulation

### Applicable Regulations

All school food procurement using federal Child Nutrition Program funds must comply with:

| Regulation | Subject | Key Requirement |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **2 CFR 200.318** | General procurement standards | Full and open competition; SFA must monitor contractor performance |
| **2 CFR 200.319** | Competition | No geographic preferences (except for unprocessed local ag products); no unnecessary restrictions |
| **2 CFR 200.320** | Methods of procurement | Defines micro-purchase, small purchase, sealed bid, competitive proposals, noncompetitive |
| **2 CFR 200.321** | Contracting with small/disadvantaged businesses | Affirmative steps to use minority, women-owned, veteran-owned businesses |
| **2 CFR 200.322** | Domestic preferences | Buy American requirements |
| **2 CFR 200.323** | Cost and price analysis | Required for every procurement action including modifications |
| **2 CFR 200.326** | Contract provisions | Required contract clauses including equal opportunity, anti-kickback, etc. |
| **7 CFR 210.21** | NSLP-specific procurement | Ties NSLP to 2 CFR 200 standards; includes Buy American provision |
| **7 CFR 210.21(d)** | Buy American Provision | Domestic commodities/products required to maximum extent practicable |
| **7 CFR 220.16** | School Breakfast procurement | Same standards as NSLP |

Source: [USDA Child Nutrition Procurement Regulations](https://www.fns.usda.gov/f2s/usda-procurement-regulations)

### Buy American Provision (7 CFR 210.21(d))

- SFAs **must purchase domestic commodities or products to the maximum extent practicable**
- **"Domestic commodity"**: Agricultural commodity produced in the United States
- **"Domestic product"**: Food product processed in the U.S. substantially using agricultural commodities produced in the U.S.
- **"Substantially"**: Over 51% of agricultural commodities must be grown domestically
- **Newport advantage**: As a Florida-based wholesaler with 35 years of domestic sourcing, Buy American compliance is a natural competitive strength
- Source: [USDA Buy American Compliance](https://www.fns.usda.gov/nslp/compliance-enforcement-buy-american)

### Geographic Preference (Limited)

- CNP operators **may voluntarily apply geographic preference** when procuring **unprocessed locally grown or locally raised agricultural products** through small purchase or formal procurement
- **Does NOT apply to processed foods** (most of what Newport would supply)
- Must still use full and open competition per 2 CFR 200.319
- **Newport advantage**: Being local in Florida is helpful but cannot be a formal evaluation criterion for processed food products
- Source: [USDA Procuring Local Foods](https://www.fns.usda.gov/f2s/procuring-local-foods)

### Procurement Thresholds (Florida School Districts, 2025-2026)

| Method | Threshold | Process |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Micro-purchase | Up to $50,000 (FL self-certified per Rule 6A-1.012(7)) | No competitive bidding required; distribute evenly among qualified vendors |
| Small purchase | $50,000 - $249,999 | Minimum 3 written quotes |
| Sealed bid (ITB) | $250,000+ (federal) / $50,000+ (some FL districts) | Formal competitive sealed bid; public advertising |
| Competitive proposals (RFP) | $250,000+ | When sealed bid not appropriate; evaluation criteria beyond price |

**Key insight for Newport**: Florida's $50,000 self-certified micro-purchase threshold is unusually high. This means many school food purchases can be made WITHOUT formal bidding — making relationship-building and being a known vendor critically important.

## 6. ITB Process

**Confidence: HIGH** — standard FL school district procurement

### Typical Step-by-Step ITB Process for Food Supply

**Phase 1: Pre-Solicitation (District Side)**
1. School food service identifies need (e.g., mainline grocery distributor, breakfast/lunch entrees)
2. Specifications developed by food service and purchasing departments
3. Bid package prepared with detailed requirements

**Phase 2: Solicitation (2-4 weeks)**
4. ITB posted publicly — typically on DemandStar, BidNet Direct, district website, and/or newspaper of general circulation
5. Advertisement period: minimum 2 weeks for sealed bids (public advertising in newspaper required)
6. Pre-bid conference (optional but common for large contracts — attendance may be mandatory)
7. Questions and addenda period
8. "Cone of Silence" — no contact with district officials about the solicitation during bidding period

**Phase 3: Bid Submission**
9. Sealed bids submitted before deadline (late bids rejected, no exceptions)
10. Bids by email, fax, or phone NOT accepted
11. Required submissions typically include:
    - Completed bid form with unit pricing
    - Signed bid acknowledgment
    - Certificate of Insurance (or carrier letter)
    - E-Verify MOU
    - Vendor affidavits (coercion, foreign concern)
    - Bid bond/deposit (if required)
    - References
    - Product samples (sometimes)

**Phase 4: Evaluation and Award (2-6 weeks)**
12. Public bid opening on scheduled date/time
13. Evaluation by food service and purchasing staff
14. ITB: Award to **lowest responsive, responsible bidder** (price is primary criterion)
15. RFP: Award based on evaluation criteria (price may be one factor among several)
16. Award recommendation posted for **72 hours** (protest period)
17. Any protest must be filed within 72 hours; formal written protest within 10 calendar days
18. School Board approval of contract

**Phase 5: Contract Execution (1-3 weeks)**
19. Successful bidder signs contract
20. Performance/payment bond furnished within 10-30 days (if required)
21. Insurance certificates verified
22. Background screening initiated for delivery personnel (if campus delivery)
23. Purchase orders issued

### Typical Contract Terms

- **Duration**: 1 year (e.g., May 1 through April 30)
- **Renewals**: 1-2 one-year renewal options upon mutual consent and School Board approval
- **Pricing**: Fixed pricing for contract period; may include escalation clauses tied to market indices
- **Delivery**: Per purchase order, to central warehouse or individual school sites
- Source: [Escambia County ITB Example](https://static.escambia.k12.fl.us/procurement/BPA/2025/Bid_250902_Breakfast_and_Lunch_Entrees.pdf)

### Product Requirements (Example: Escambia County)

- All shelf-stable goods must have minimum **6-month shelf life** at delivery
- **Best By Dates must be clearly marked** on side of boxes or pallets
- Products must comply with USDA commodity specifications where applicable
- Buy American provision applies

## 7. Past Performance

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — varies by procurement method and district

### Key Facts

- **Past performance CAN be used as an evaluation criterion** as long as the SFA informs vendors it is one of the criteria
- **For ITBs (sealed bids)**: Award goes to lowest responsive, RESPONSIBLE bidder — "responsible" includes capacity and past performance assessment
- **For RFPs**: Past performance is commonly weighted 10-25% of total evaluation score
- A vendor's past performance evaluation helps determine whether a bidder is **"responsible"** (capable of performing)

### Impact on New/First-Time Bidders

- **No FL statute prohibits first-time bidders** from winning school food contracts
- Districts evaluate "responsibility" — which includes financial capacity, infrastructure, and ability to perform
- **Newport's 35 years of wholesale distribution is highly relevant past performance** even without prior school district contracts
- References from commercial customers (restaurants, grocers, institutions) demonstrate capability
- **Strategy for Newport**: Emphasize warehouse infrastructure, fleet, cold chain capability, FL-based operations, and 35-year track record of reliable distribution

### What Strengthens a First-Time School District Bid

1. Commercial customer references (restaurants, hotels, institutions — similar delivery profiles)
2. Dun & Bradstreet report showing financial stability
3. Evidence of temperature-controlled warehouse and fleet
4. Documented food safety program (HACCP plan, GMP procedures)
5. Proof of Buy American compliance capability
6. SBE/MBE certification (if applicable)

## 8. Vehicle & Fleet Requirements

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — based on USDA food safety standards and FL regulations

### Temperature Control Requirements

| Product Category | Required Temperature | Standard |
|-----------------|---------------------|----------|
| Refrigerated products | 41 degrees F or below | FDA Food Code / USDA |
| Frozen products | 0 degrees F or below | FDA Food Code / USDA |
| Hot foods (if applicable) | 135 degrees F or above | FDA Food Code |

### Vehicle Standards

- **Delivery vehicles must be of sturdy construction** with:
  - Easy rear- and side-door locking and sealing
  - Sufficient insulation and refrigeration to protect cargo
  - Interior walls and floors clean and free of cracks/holes
  - No pest, vermin, or dust entry points
  - Commercial-grade refrigeration equipment
- **Best practice**: Automatic time/temperature recording devices (data loggers)
- **In-transit temperature checks** expected by leading districts
- Source: [Food Safety Magazine - Cold Chain](https://www.food-safety.com/articles/4541-foodservice-distribution-maintaining-the-cold-chain)

### Delivery Schedule Requirements (Typical)

- **Warehouse delivery**: Monday-Friday, typically 7:30 AM - 2:30 PM
- **School site delivery**: May require early morning delivery before school starts (6:00 AM - 8:00 AM in some districts)
- **Lead time**: Typically 24-48 hours from purchase order
- **Frequency**: 1-3 deliveries per week depending on district size and agreement
- **Minimum order**: May be specified in contract (varies)

### Newport Advantage

Newport already operates refrigerated trucks and warehouse with cold chain — this is a significant infrastructure advantage over new market entrants. Key: ensure all vehicles have current DOT inspection, temperature monitoring documentation, and clean appearance.

## 10. Background Checks & Fingerprinting

**Confidence: HIGH** — FL Statute 1012.467 (Jessica Lunsford Act)

### Who Must Be Screened

Per **Florida Statute 1012.467**, any "noninstructional contractor" — defined as any vendor, individual, or entity under contract with a school or school board — AND their employees and subcontractors who:

- Are permitted access on school grounds when students are present
- Have direct or indirect contact with students
- Have access to or control of school funds

**This includes food delivery drivers who deliver to school sites during school hours.**

### Level 2 Background Screening Process

1. **Submit required forms** (Clearinghouse Request for Information Form, Privacy Policy)
2. **Schedule fingerprinting appointment** via www.fieldprintflorida.com (district-specific code required)
3. **Fingerprints processed** through FDLE (state) and FBI (national) criminal history check
4. **Badge issued** upon clearance — typically 10 business days processing
5. **Badge must be worn visibly** at all times on school property

### Costs

| Item | Cost (Approximate) |
|------|--------------------|
| Level 2 background check + badge | $83.25 - $94.75 per person |
| Missed appointment rescheduling | $15.00 |
| Payment | Credit card typically required |

Source: [St. Johns County Screening Process](https://www.stjohns.k12.fl.us/screening/process/)

### Badge Duration & Renewal

- **Valid for 5 years** from original fingerprinting date
- **Statewide validity** — badge issued by one district is recognized by others (statewide photo ID badge system)
- Must be re-fingerprinted and badged every 5 years
- Source: [Pinellas County Schools](https://www.pcsb.org/departments/facilities-and-operations/pinellas-county-schools-police/level-2-screeningcontractor-badge-instructions)

### Disqualifying Offenses

Vendors cannot have convictions for (partial list):
- Sexual offenses or registration violations
- Murder, kidnapping
- Child abuse or neglect
- Terrorism
- Lewdness/indecent exposure

### Reporting Obligations

- Contractors must report arrests for disqualifying offenses **within 48 hours** — failure is a third-degree felony

### Critical Newport Consideration: Warehouse vs. School Delivery

- **If Newport delivers ONLY to a district central warehouse** (not to individual schools), background screening may not be required — the statute applies to access on "school grounds when students are present"
- **A district warehouse is not a school** — this is a potential exemption, but districts interpret this differently
- **Conservative approach**: Get all delivery drivers screened regardless — it eliminates a barrier and shows commitment
- **Example**: Escambia County food deliveries go to Central Warehouse at 51 E Texar Drive — not to schools. This model avoids Jessica Lunsford Act requirements for delivery personnel.
- **Recommendation for Newport**: Negotiate warehouse-only delivery terms when possible; get key drivers screened for flexibility

## 11. Cooperative Purchasing Vehicles

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH** — well-documented programs

### A. BuyBoard (Florida School Boards Association)

- **Affiliation**: National cooperative, endorsed by FSBA
- **Eligibility**: Must be awarded a BuyBoard contract through competitive procurement
- **Process**: Wait for relevant food service contract to come up for bid, download specs from www.vendor.buyboard.com, submit proposal
- **Contract duration**: Approximately 3 years
- **Timeline risk**: Could be 2-3 years before next food service bid opens
- **Key insight**: Product/commodity-based contracts, not project-based
- **Contact**: 800-695-2919
- Source: [BuyBoard Vendor Registration](https://www.buyboard.com/vendor/how-to-register)

### B. Florida Buy (State Cooperative Purchasing)

- **Affiliation**: Legislatively sanctioned; managed by Panhandle (PAEC), Heartland (HEC), and North East Florida (NEFEC) consortia
- **Legal basis**: Satisfies FS 1010.04(1)(b) requirement for Florida schools to use cooperative state purchasing
- **Free statewide solution** for government procurement
- **Includes food-related contracts** among thousands of products/services
- **Process**: Respond to competitively solicited RFPs through the Association of Educational Purchasing Agencies
- Source: [Florida Buy](https://floridabuy.org/)

### C. Other Cooperative Vehicles

| Cooperative | Scope | Food Contracts? | How to Join |
|------------|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| OMNIA Partners | National; K-12 focus | Yes — food service categories | Respond to cooperative RFP when posted |
| Sourcewell | National; 50,000+ members | Limited food | Respond to cooperative solicitation |
| US Communities (now OMNIA Public Sector) | National | Yes | Merged with OMNIA |

### D. Piggybacking

- FL school districts can **piggyback on existing contracts** from other government agencies
- Agencies that piggyback are bound by the original terms, conditions, and pricing
- **If Newport wins one school district contract, others may piggyback on it** — creating cascading opportunities
- Also can piggyback on state contracts (MFMP) and other cooperative contracts

### Strategy for Newport

1. **Register on DemandStar and BidNet Direct immediately** — monitor for food supply bids
2. **Register on MFMP** — opens state contract piggyback opportunities
3. **Target direct ITBs from individual districts first** — fastest path to first contract
4. **Apply for BuyBoard and Florida Buy** when food service solicitations open — longer play but massive reach
5. **Once you win one district, leverage it** — other districts can piggyback or reference it

## 12. Small vs. Large District Differences

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — based on analysis of publicly available district information

### Small/Rural Districts (e.g., Lafayette, Franklin, Bay County)

| Factor | Small District Characteristic |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Annual food spend | $500K - $5M |
| Procurement threshold | $50,000 (self-certified micro-purchase) |
| Bid process | Less formal; may use informal quotes under threshold |
| Bonding | Rarely required for food supply contracts |
| Insurance minimums | Standard $1M CGL |
| Background checks | Still required per state law, but smaller scale |
| Delivery model | Central warehouse delivery common |
| Decision-makers | Food service director + superintendent (small team) |
| Competition | Less competitive; fewer vendors willing to service rural areas |
| Contract size | Smaller but easier to win |
| Relationship importance | Very high — personal relationships matter significantly |

### Large Districts (e.g., Broward, Miami-Dade, Palm Beach)

| Factor | Large District Characteristic |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Annual food spend | $50M - $200M+ |
| Procurement threshold | Formal ITB/RFP process for most purchases |
| Bid process | Highly formal; complex solicitations, multiple evaluation criteria |
| Bonding | More likely required; performance bonds at 100% |
| Insurance minimums | Higher — may require $2M-$5M umbrella |
| Background checks | Required; high volume of personnel to screen |
| Delivery model | May require delivery to individual school sites (50-300+ schools) |
| Decision-makers | Large procurement departments; committee-based evaluation |
| Competition | Very competitive; Sysco, US Foods, PFG, local distributors all bidding |
| Contract size | Very large but highly competitive |
| Relationship importance | Process-driven; relationships matter less than compliance and price |

### Recommended Newport Entry Strategy

**Start with mid-size districts in South Florida:**

**Then expand to:**
- Small rural districts (easy wins, build past performance)

## 14. FDACS School Nutrition Requirements

**Confidence: HIGH** — state agency requirements

### FDACS Role

The Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services, Division of Food, Nutrition and Wellness, administers child nutrition programs for the State of Florida, including:

- National School Lunch Program (NSLP)
- School Breakfast Program (SBP)
- Summer Food Service Program (SFSP)
- Child and Adult Care Food Program (CACFP)

### Vendor Requirements Through FDACS

1. **Complete vendor information sheet** ("Child Nutrition Programs Food Service Vendor Information Sheet")
2. **Provide Florida business license** (as restaurant, caterer, or food service management company — or wholesale food permit for distributors)
3. **Complete online vendor training** at https://agacademy.fdacs.gov/ (minimum 80% score)
4. **Provide Sunbiz registration**
5. **Submit to FDACS** to be added to vendor list

### FDACS Notification Requirements

- Sponsors (school districts) must notify FDACS **at least 14 days before a bid opening totaling $100,000 or more**
- Must provide time, place of bid opening, and contact information
- Must submit copy of IFB packet to FDACS prior to beginning of program operations

### USDA Foods Distribution

- FDACS coordinates distribution of USDA commodity foods to school districts
- School districts receive donated USDA commodities (entitlement foods) that supplement purchased food
- Vendors may be contracted to **process USDA commodities** (e.g., converting raw chicken into chicken nuggets)
- Processing USDA commodities is a separate, more complex qualification process

- Division of Food, Nutrition and Wellness
- Phone: (850) 617-7400
